Picopacket

The Picopacket breaks new ground in terms of both size and performance. Although actually slightly smaller than a packet of cigarettes the Pico offers the same Packet performance as it's larger brothers such as the Tiny 2 including support for GPS making it ideal for mobile tracking applications where space is critical.

TAPR based firmware.

(100% TNC-2 compatible).

Powerful battery backed PMS (Personal Message System) compatible with most major international BBS programs for automatic mail delivery and collection.

7 Z181 microprocessor.

32K battery backed RAM (expandable to 128K or 512K).

1200 baud VHF modem (TCM 3105).

GPS support as per the Tiny 2/KPC-3.

Extensive surface mount technology.

Supporting software for virtually any popular computer available from AAPRA.

Low power consumption <45mA.

Size 1" x 2.5" x 3"

Weight 170g! (No, that's not a typing mistake!.)

Supplied with ready made computer cable for your setup.

Heavy duty aluminium extruded cabinet.

100+ page user manual.

Tiny 2 MK II

The Tiny 2 is often regarded as a World-wide standard for Packet Radio operation. With over 14,000 in use the Tiny enjoys tremendous hardware and software support.

We are presently supplying the MK 2 version which offers extra features over and above the original design including support for GPS (Global Positioning System) interfaces.

The Tiny is also used extensively in the ROSE network.

SPRINT-2

The PacComm Sprint-2 is a third generation High-Speed Packet Controller capable of 9.6 - 64k operation by simply moving internal jumper links making it the ideal choice for both terrestrial and Satellite users. It's worth noting that this is one of the ever growing range of products to use the tried and tested G3RUH design offering superior performance over and above existing 9600 designs.

SPECIFICATIONS:

TAPR TNC-2 based firmware offering 100% compatibility with TheNet, BPQ Kiss, WA8DED, NordLink etc.

G3RUH Technology verses other designs..

Other manufacturers use either a single chip or 'simplified' G3RUH design (usually unlicensed) to keep the component coasts down. Whilst such approaches will perform fine on the bench and in a 5+9 link situation it just doesn't happen like that in the real world and these cheaper imitations soon reveal their true worth.
